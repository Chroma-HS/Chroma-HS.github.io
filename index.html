Abstract

Head swapping is a task that generates the seamless results of replacing the target human's head with the source head, which can be used in various industrial scenarios. However, the state-of-the-art head swapping framework (HeSer) sometimes shows low-fidelity artifacts on 1) the background and 2) the body and neck regions, resulting in flaw production. The area that shows artifacts are hidden in both the source and the target due to the difference between head shapes and the long hair of the target.

Therefore, we propose Chroma-HS, a framework that transfers the head swapping task to a chroma key setting, which is a cost-effective way to remove artifacts on the inpainted background region and build a flawless system for industrial applications. Although adapting the chroma keying resolves the artifacts on the background, the artifacts on the body and the neck remain. Thus, we introduce Actor Manipulation and Mask-Prediction and -Aware Transformer (MPAT). Actor Manipulation is designed to reduce the artifacts due to the train-test gap. The train-test gap occurs because the head swap model is trained in a self-supervised way with the same identity of the source and the target, and it can not cover the cross-identity setting in the test. Actor Manipulation mitigates the problem by applying various augmentations to the model's input during training. MPAT inpaints the body and the neck region by only using the unmasked area of the body and the neck, not other parts such as the background. Experimental results show that our Chroma-HS significantly outperforms the state-of-the-art head swapping model on benchmark datasets qualitatively and quantitatively. Moreover, we demonstrate the effectiveness of our model using extensive in-depth studies.

Motivations

Chroma-HS is proposed in consideration of the real-world application due to imperfect inpainting problems of previous work [2] (HeSer) such as red boxes of (a). To do so, Chroma-HS paints the background of the target with the green screen for chroma keying. However, it still remains low-fidelity results to inpaint the neck and the body which is hidden in the target image due to head shape difference and the long hair described in a red box of (b). The blue box of (b) shows our high-fidelity results corresponding region to the red box of (b). Finally, Chroma-HS removes artifacts on the backgrounds through the chroma key technique as shown in the blue boxes of (c), and easily inserts various high-fidelity real-world backgrounds.

Methods

Chroma-HS consists of an encoder (E), Head Colorizer blocks, Body Blender blocks including Mask-Prediction and -Aware Transformer (MPAT) modules, which are denoted with red arrows, and a decoder (D). We visualize our proposed input (X) manipulation method (blue arrows) with overall training and inference scheme. The Head Colorizer colorizes the gray head of X, and the Body Blender inpaints the hidden body and the neck with a mask-aware attention mechanism.
